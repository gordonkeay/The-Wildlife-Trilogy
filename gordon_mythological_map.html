The Mythological Map

Every symbol, archetype and mythic theme from the writing of Gordon Keay

These are not borrowed symbols. Every image on this map arrived through your own writing — through the dream-time diaries, the voice recordings, the vigil, the dog walks. They are the mythic layer already present in a life fully and honestly lived. The map is here so you can see the whole constellation at once and understand what the writing already knows.
〜
Water, The Deep & The Threshold
The Lake as Teacher
Lac Léman as the primary mythic presence. Patient, ancient, indifferent to human drama. The water that receives without judgment. The teacher that asks only for attention.
"The lake is the teacher. We are all still the students."
Archeus Paramanta Wildlife
The Underworld Dive
The descent into the dark water — bioluminescent, primordial, terrifying. The classic hero's katabasis. Going down to retrieve what was lost. The vigil passage as full mythic underworld journey.
"When a man falls into the underworld, he does so at great risk. Not just anyone is able to visit."
Wildlife Archeus
The Breach
Rising from the deep into the air. The whale breach. The foil breach. The moment of emergence after the underworld journey. Seventy five centimetres above the water — the threshold between worlds.
"I hit the light with a force. I breached. I soared out from the deep and flew into the sky."
Paramanta Wildlife
Two Boys In Water
Rupert who sank with curiosity and turned blue. Gordon who breached like a cork, fighting. The temperament of each revealed at the threshold between life and death.
"The sinker and the surface fighter."
Wildlife
The Reef Edge
The boundary between the sunlit reef and the dark fathomless deep. The place of vertigo and terror and initiation. Swimming to the edge and looking down.
"The dark danger that looks so intoxicating and draws you over and down."
Wildlife Archeus
Bioluminescence
Light made by movement in the dark water. The teaching of the underworld — without perpetual motion the light goes out. The hands creating light by dancing through the primordial soup.
"I learned to dance in the deep making light."
Wildlife Paramanta

Fire, Light & The Flame
The Candle Vigil
The recurring practice of writing by candlelight before dawn. The flame as sacred witness. The pen, paper, candle and verveine as the four alchemical elements assembled for purification.
"The pen hard metal, the inky water element, the paper earth element, the flickering fire element."
Wildlife Archeus
The Candle Falls
The candle sliding from the desk and shattering at the exact moment of approaching the truth about sexuality. Darkness as initiation. The broken vessel. Finding a match. Light remade by bare hands.
"The candle searching for the glide."
Wildlife
The Salamander
Paracelsus's fire spirit. The purifier. The creature that lives in flame without being consumed. The lizard found in the pond behind Dolores's house in Tolmin — real and symbolic simultaneously.
"The salamander, the fire spirit, the purifier."
Archeus Wildlife
Making Fire from Nothing
The flint struck against the watch back. The nest of dried grass. The ember nursed to flame. The ancient skill of making light when all modern methods fail. Fire built from knowledge not equipment.
"Just when thoughts of failure were creeping in — just then, at this point where it is the darkest — there was light."
Archeus Wildlife
The Venice Beach Candle
The fortune teller. The candle practice given to a young man before Fiji. Gratitude by firelight every night for two months. The first intentional practice. The origin of the dream-time vigils.
"I became a hunter for the joy lying await at all times if we can just tune in enough to see it."
Wildlife Archeus

The Pearl, Alchemy & Transformation
Grit to Pearl
The central alchemical symbol of the trilogy. Grit — suffering, pressure, the wound — as the seed of beauty. The pearl built layer by layer in darkness. Solve et coagula. The base matter becoming the precious.
"Pearls are made of grit. Grit centred, built slowly, layer after layer, from the inside out."
Wildlife Archeus Paramanta
The Pearl Farm
Broome, Western Australia. Real pearls washed off the deck by a casual wave. Beauty lost in nonchalance. The experience that embedded the pearl symbol in the body before it became metaphor.
"I saw beauty lost in such nonchalance and it changed me."
Wildlife
The Giant Clam
The underworld encounter. The tightest clam that had to be wooed to open. The feminine mystery. The pearl inside that was promised not to be taken. The reflection of the self finally seen inside her walls.
"I had to woo her, to tickle her in just the right places. She had to know I was not going to steal her pearl."
Wildlife
The Huntress & The Pearl Necklace
The goddess who takes the pearl, pierces it with a hunting knife and threads it onto her necklace. The initiatrix who takes innocence and adds it to her collection. Dolores as mythic feminine force.
"She puts on her pearl necklace, smiles and cackles as another innocence has been taken."
Wildlife
Bread as Alchemy
Flour, salt, water, heat — base elements transformed. The fire, the purification, the transfiguration. The bread makes the baker as the baker makes the bread. Daily alchemy as spiritual practice.
"In this way the bread makes me as I bake it. We dance in a circularity."
Archeus Wildlife
Paracelsus
The 500 year old Swiss alchemist as ancestral presence. Archeus — the inner architect, the vital healing force. The man who walked out into nature because nature held all secrets. Solve et coagula as the philosophy of the whole project.
"Books be damned. To the fire with your theories, go out and live it."
Archeus Paramanta

The Journey, The Exile & Finding Home
The Homing Pigeon
The boy of twelve who memorised every turning on a road driven once and navigated home by instinct alone. The recurring pattern of escape and return. The whole life as navigation back to the self before the severance.
"Like a homing pigeon my dad found me hours later sitting on the roadside throwing pebbles into a pot."
Wildlife
The Severance
Eight years old. The trunk with name tags sewn into every sock. Boarding school. The wound at the origin of everything. The moment the voice was clipped and the map was taken away.
"Banished from the kingdom without a tongue and no map or compass."
Wildlife
The Great Escape
Three escapes: St Anselm's via the cricket pavilion. The farm at twelve. The boat in Broome. Steve McQueen throwing his ball. The recurring refusal to be contained. The wild soul that cannot be domesticated.
"I made a great escape and I almost got home."
Wildlife
Linguistic Exile
Twenty years in France and Switzerland. The voice that could tell jokes and hold rooms suddenly unable to carry across a language barrier. The compounding of the original wound. The expat vacuum.
"A pearl lost on the top deck, washed with the waves and on the way over the deck and out to sea."
Wildlife
Grimsby to Geneva
The arc the life draws on a map. The same arc Lac Léman draws. The world's largest fishing fleet to the shore of an Alpine lake. The journey that could not have been any other way.
"From Grimsby to Geneva, following the same arc the Léman draws on a map."
Wildlife Archeus
Wildlife — Finding My Way Home
The title as mythic statement. Not wild life as nature. Wildlife as the state of being thrown into the world without a map and having to navigate back by instinct alone. The whole life as homecoming.
"Just trying to find your way home after this wildlife."
Wildlife

The Creature Teachers
The Manta Ray — Paramanta
The master of glide. The teacher. The guru. Paramahansa and Paracelsus and Manta Ray unified in one word. The creature that moves without effort, that embodies the art of glide as its natural state.
"He isn't searching for the glide. He is the glide. The master glidition."
Paramanta Archeus
The Nudibranch
The sea slug with lungs on the outside. Naked lunged. The reward of slowing down enough to truly see. The nudibranch lens — full beam attention on the small beautiful things. I was the nudibranch.
"The more you pay attention the more you find. Attention is a gift to channel into the little things."
Archeus Wildlife Paramanta
The Proboscis Monkey
Beauty that demolishes Western standards. The creature who inhabits himself completely without apology. The magnificent ridiculous nose worn with total dignity. The antidote to shame and self-consciousness.
"I am becoming proboscis with bonobo tendencies with manta soul and nudi eyes."
Wildlife Archeus
The Bonobo
The primate led by love not dominance. Connection through the body without shame. The alternative to chimp aggression and toxic masculinity. Bonobo tendencies as a philosophy of relating.
"I have always been more bonobo than chimp."
Wildlife
Scamper
The white jack russell. The first love. The one who never hurt on the deep level. The unconditional gaze. The animal who saw Gordon clearly and loved what he saw without reservation or condition.
"You were my first love. You never hurt me. Not on the deep level. Not like human hurt."
Wildlife
The Worm
The literal parasite in Fiji and the cultural parasite in the mind. The thing that diminishes from the inside. The coloniser of the sacred biome. Also — without the worm there is no pearl. The wound and the gift as one.
"I was being diminished from the inside. The worm was taking over, in control of the ship."
Wildlife
The Homing Pigeon
Navigation by instinct. Memory of every turning on a road driven once. The animal intelligence that knows the way home even when the conscious mind has no map. The body carrying what the mind forgot.
"Homing Pigeon Logic."
Wildlife
The Grebe
The bird that reads water with complete attention. The first teacher in Archeus. The creature that taught Archeus to see — to stand still long enough to notice what was always there.
"The grebe taught me to see."
Archeus

The Feminine, The Masculine & The Yin Yang
Gordana
The feminine form of Gordon. The part that wore the dresses and felt elegant and sexy for the first time at forty five. The retrieved part. The other half of the yin yang finally named and welcomed home.
"I want to welcome Gordana gently into the world. To invite her in."
Wildlife
Dolores — The Huntress Goddess
Leather and green. Black boots. Long black hair like the inky black of the deep. The initiatrix. The one whose language he didn't know. The encounter that broke the old self open. The pearl that was pierced.
"She who spoke the old language. Does a goddess need an excuse to take a mortal for her pleasure?"
Wildlife
The Yin Yang — The 69
The masculine and feminine entwined in eternal embrace. Each containing a seed of the other. The philosophical architecture of the whole trilogy. Surface and deep. Fighter and thinker. Gordon and Gordana.
"The cosmic sixty nine. To welcome the feminine into the masculine. To interlock the two."
Wildlife Archeus Paramanta
The Cuckold
Hephaestus at his forge. The man who loves most completely and is most vulnerable to loss. Not humiliation but transformation. The maker who outlasts the warrior. The naive cuckold who went to Vipassana and came back changed.
"The naive cuckhold... shaped in the cold darkness, under pressure."
Wildlife
The Cherry Blossom
Beauty fragile and seasonal. The cold snap that threatens to freeze the flower before it blooms. The feminine form luscious and overflowing in rich giving bounty. Beauty that requires the right conditions to survive.
"The cold frost snaps her flowery thong... timing is everything."
Wildlife Archeus

The Shadow, The Wound & The Ogre
Inherited Silence
The great grandfather whose portrait hung on the wall but whose story was never told. The silence that moved from generation to generation. The cycle of repression and mutism. The chain being broken.
"The cycle of inherited trauma stops here. It ends with me."
Wildlife
The Ogre
The stepfather who tore the world apart. The monster in the castle. The source of cortisol and fear and the need for armour. Named, held, eventually forgiven — not because he deserved it but because the hatred was no longer serving.
"You were the monster. You scared me and I hated you for tearing my world apart."
Wildlife
The Clipped Tongue
Children should be seen and not heard. The voice suppressed before it could develop. The mutism. The linguistic exile. Mr Malcolm hearing it anyway. The whole project as the restoration of the clipped tongue.
"When your tongue was cut off and you were cast off before you were shaped, left in the wilds."
Wildlife
The Topsy Turvy World
The world that has inverted its own logic. Sunscreen causes cancer. Cleaning products cause lung cancer. Social media is anti-social. The worm inside the mind. The cultural parasite that profits from insecurity.
"We ride electric bikes and lose our power. Then go to a gym to get it back."
Archeus Wildlife
The Armour
The protective structure built in childhood that became a prison. Fight, flight or freeze as a way of life. The man who had to be guarded to survive environments that were hostile to his true nature.
"Your guard goes up, your armour on and you fight, flight or freeze your way through life."
Wildlife

The Tools & The Making
The Foil
The tool that shapes the rider as the rider shapes it. The practice that teaches what no teacher could say. The dock start as initiation. A thousand falls as the path to the glide. The manta ray out of water made useful.
"When we shape it, it will shape us. If the wand chooses the wizard, does the foil choose the rider?"
Paramanta Archeus Wildlife
The Bent Nib
The pen that flew from the hand and hit the bin at the exact moment of approaching the truth. The nib bent permanently. The tool shaped by the moment of contact with the truth. Carrying the scar of the important night.
"The tools that shape we shape end up shaping us. The nib of my pen now bent at an angle, never to be the same again."
Wildlife
The Stone Effect
Not the random butterfly effect but the intentional stone on water. Visible ripples. Concentric circles of ever expanding reach. The choice made in the here and now to give yourself to a project bigger than you know.
"This isn't the butterfly effect, that's too random. This is the stone effect."
Archeus Paramanta Wildlife
The Drum
The heartbeat as drum. Martin Shaw's blue Tibetan tent. The Balinese gamelan. The drum that only stops beating when the light goes out. Dancing to the beat of your own drum as the philosophy of perpetual motion.
"The drum only stops beating when the light goes out. So I had to keep dancing."
Paramanta Wildlife
The Leading Edge
The foil's most vulnerable and most consequential surface. The place where magic happens. The leading edge hit on a rock — the bent nib. Protect the leading edge as a philosophy of life and craft.
"I hit the leading edge on a rock. The leading edge, where the magic happens."
Paramanta Wildlife

On cherche le glisse
We search for the glide. The through line of the whole trilogy. Not a destination but a practice. The motto of a life that began in a mustard field in Lincolnshire and arrived on the shore of Lac Léman.
Paramanta Archeus Wildlife
The Love Letter
The whole project understood completely. A love letter to the self. To the eight year old by his trunk. To Scamper. To the lake. To all the beautiful souls carried to the candle. A letter of gratitude to all the people who have been there.
"This is a love letter to me for me."
Wildlife Archeus Paramanta
The Soul Family
Soiley Whitting's teaching. The people who vibrate at the same frequency. Darrell Tatum, Bob the panel beater, the ISFIT friends, Mr Malcolm, the skipper in Broome. Carried to the candle. Treasures. People are treasures.
"We are part of a soul family and those soul families will stay together regardless of time or space."
Wildlife
The Three Candles
The vigil. Three flames lit for the victims. The eight year old, the silenced ones, the dispossessed. The ceremony of retrieval. The sacred space created before dawn for the healing work.
"I held a vigil for all of the victims. I sat attentive with three candles lit and invited them to sit with me."
Wildlife
